Common Livestock Issues in Eastern Africa

  • East Coast Fever: Tick-borne disease affecting cattle, causing fever, lymph node swelling, and high mortality.
  • Rift Valley Fever: Viral disease affecting cattle, sheep, goats, and humans; causes abortions and high mortality in young animals.
  • Brucellosis: Bacterial infection causing abortions, infertility, and reduced milk production in cattle, goats, and sheep.
  • Mastitis: Inflammation of the udder, leading to decreased milk yield and quality in dairy animals.
  • Trypanosomiasis: Parasitic disease transmitted by tsetse flies, causing anemia, weight loss, and death in cattle.
  • Foot-and-Mouth Disease: Highly contagious viral disease causing blisters, fever, and lameness in cloven-hoofed animals.
  • Newcastle Disease: Viral disease affecting poultry, causing respiratory distress, nervous signs, and high mortality.
  • Parasitic Infestations: Ticks, worms, and flies are major causes of disease and production loss in livestock.

Our veterinary team is experienced in managing these and other livestock health challenges, providing tailored solutions for farmers in Eastern Africa.
